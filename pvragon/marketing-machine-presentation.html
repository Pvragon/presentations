The problem

Product is no longer the moat.

In a world where agentic development lets anyone build a production-ready product in days, the differentiator shifts downstream.

  • Code is commoditized. LLMs + agents can assemble the same features we can, at 1/10th the time and cost.
  • Features are copyable. What ships on Monday gets cloned by Friday.
  • Attention is the scarce resource. The winners aren't who builds better — they're who gets heard.
The opportunity

Distribution is the new moat.

Whoever can reliably reach the most customers, in the most channels, with the most relevant message, wins.

But there's a catch: distribution has traditionally required headcount — SDRs, content teams, media buyers, PR, community managers. Doing it all well has meant hiring 20+ people for a year before you see results.

What if a small team could run distribution at the scale of a 50-person marketing org?

The proposal

Build a machine, not a team.

A unified go-to-market system that:

  • Covers every viable marketing channel a company might want to use.
  • Operates on agentic automation, with humans in the loop only where judgment is irreplaceable.
  • Starts producing revenue in 60–90 days, not 18 months.
  • Scales to 10x output without 10x the headcount.

This deck lays out the framework, the methodology, and the first 11 things we build.

Methodology · Step 4

Sequence by revenue velocity.

Not every capability should be built at once. We grouped the 51 nodes into 8 phases, ordered by how quickly they produce revenue:

P0 Revenue Now P1 Foundation P2 Paid Acquisition P3 Content Engine P4 Organic Distribution P5 Earned P6 Rich Media & Events P7 Specialized

P0 is the fastest direct path to a closed deal. P1 is the measurement and infrastructure backbone everything else needs. P2–P7 are the compounding layers you add once revenue is flowing.

Methodology · Step 5

Different businesses, different sequences.

A B2B enterprise play shouldn't build the same machine as a content business or a print-on-demand shop. We encoded 11 business-type presets:

  • B2B SaaS (micro)
  • B2C SaaS (micro)
  • Print on Demand
  • Printables (digital)
  • Custom Directory
  • High-Value Marketplace
  • Database Business
  • B2B Services
  • 1st-Party Small Products
  • B2C Microtools
  • Content Business

Each preset re-weights channel effectiveness and re-sequences the phases so the first 10 things you build actually match how that business makes money.

The Baseline P0

What we build first.

For a generic B2B SaaS, here are the 11 capabilities that get built in phase zero — in this exact order.

The order matters: each step builds on the outputs of the ones before it. Step 1 has no dependencies. Step 11 cannot exist without steps 1–10.

Time to first revenue

With the P0 set in place, a B2B SaaS should be booking demos and closing deals in 30–60 days.

BS 1
N-013
Brand asset library
What it does

The canonical source for how the brand looks and sounds — logos, colors, fonts, voice rules. Every other node reads from here to stay on-brand.

Why it's P0 #1

Every downstream node that produces customer-facing output — copy, design, landing pages, emails, social posts — inherits from brand. If it's not defined first, everything below carries inconsistency. And zero cost to delay: your first outbound email is already making a brand impression whether you meant it or not. Define once; re-use everywhere.

BS 2
N-041
Pixel and tracking
What it does

Deploys the tracking tags on every page and conversion event — Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, UTMs, server-side events. The foundation for all downstream measurement.

Why it's P0 #2

Every paid dollar spent before pixels are live is spent blind. Without conversion tracking you have no attribution, no bid optimization, no A/B significance — just vibes. Fifteen minutes of Tag Manager configuration up front prevents three months of "we don't know what's working."

BS 3
N-037
Analytics and attribution
What it does

Measures everything. Tracks every click, conversion, and dollar across every channel. The feedback loop that makes every other node smarter.

Why it's P0 #3

Answers the question "is any of this working?" Analytics is a force multiplier — it appears in 107 of 120 channel subsystems because every measurable channel feeds it. Once pixels are live (BS 2), analytics turns signal into decisions. Without it, every later node is a bet without a scoreboard.

BS 4
N-042
Compliance and legal
What it does

Keeps every outbound message legal — CAN-SPAM, TCPA, GDPR, CCPA. Manages consent records, opt-outs, and regional routing.

Why it's P0 #4

Cold email (BS 8), SMS (future phase), and lifecycle email (P1) are all regulated channels. A compliance mistake is an existential risk — fines, deliverability collapse, or worse. Cost to implement correctly up front is tiny; cost to retrofit after a violation is catastrophic. Non-optional infrastructure, not optional hygiene.

BS 5
N-021
CRM and lifecycle state
What it does

The source of truth for every person and company we've touched — contact info, lifecycle stage, every interaction logged. HubSpot / Salesforce / Attio-style.

Why it's P0 #5

Connective tissue for sales AND lifecycle marketing. SDR cadence, email orchestration, demo delivery, review solicitation, meeting booking — every downstream node reads CRM state. Without it, you can't dedupe outbound, target lifecycle sends, or track pipeline. Not exciting to build, but required.

BS 6
N-019
Prospect list building
What it does

Builds lists of target prospects with enriched contact info — names, titles, emails, company data. Apollo / ZoomInfo / Clay-style.

Why it's P0 #6

Cold outbound is impossible without a list. List quality is the single biggest driver of cold email reply rates — the best sequence writing in the world fails against a bad list. Builds the who-to-email dataset that BS 8 cold email depends on.

BS 7
N-002
Short-form copy generation
What it does

Writes all the short text in marketing — ad headlines, social posts, email subject lines, push notifications. Tiny, punchy, everywhere.

Why it's P0 #7

The payload for every other channel that moves. Cold emails, social posts, paid ads — all are short copy at the surface. Cheapest node to build (mostly LLM + brand-voice prompt), highest channel coverage (35+ channels need it). Highest ROI node in the entire machine at 115.

BS 8
N-025
Cold email outbound
What it does

Sends cold email sequences to prospects who've never heard of you. Smartlead / Instantly / Lemlist-style deliverability-optimized outbound at scale.

Why it's P0 #8

The first active revenue-producing channel in the sequence. Takes list (BS 6) + CRM (BS 5) + copy (BS 7) + compliance (BS 4) and converts them into booked meetings. For B2B SaaS, cold email is the fastest path from "we exist" to "we closed a deal." Everything upstream exists to make this work.

BS 9
N-045
SDR cadence orchestration
What it does

Orchestrates multi-touch outbound sequences — email + LinkedIn + phone — spaced over days or weeks. What an SDR rep does, but automated.

Why it's P0 #9

Cold email alone gets ~1–3% reply rates. Multi-touch cadence (email + LinkedIn + call) raises that to 5–10% — a 3–5x multiplier on outbound pipeline. Cadence is the mechanism that turns a single-touch send into a real pipeline-generation engine. Outreach / Salesloft are commodities; the automation layer is where we win.

BS 10
N-046
Meeting booking
What it does

Calendar scheduling, reminders, timezone handling, round-robin routing across a sales team. Calendly / Chili Piper / Cal.com-style.

Why it's P0 #10

The handoff from marketing/SDR to sales. Without frictionless self-serve booking, prospect replies die in "let's find a time" ping-pong. Trivial to build (commoditized SaaS), but eliminates a massive drop-off between "interested prospect" and "meeting on the calendar."

BS 11
N-047
Demo delivery
What it does

Conducts live product demos — humans augmented by Gong-style call intelligence, or AI sales agents for simpler demos. Records, transcribes, drives follow-ups.

Why it's P0 #11

The final conversion surface where a qualified meeting becomes a closed deal. Everything in BS 1–10 existed to feed this node quality pipeline. It's the highest unit-cost node in the machine (augmentation > full automation today) — but it's where revenue actually happens, so it's non-negotiable in the baseline.
